As co-founder at Zippy Brain, a cross-platform mobile application design and development company, I act as a product manager. I assume the roles of strategist, designer, analyst, and project manager. I work closely with our developer to define features and requirements, create product roadmaps, bring products to market, and monitor existing products.

ENTERPRISE APPLICATIONS

Delivered

Merge Healthcare App Prototype

Merge Healthcare - acquired by IBM for $1 billion in October 2015 - partnered with Zippy Brain to develop an app prototype to showcase at RSNA 2015. The goal of the app is to provide Merge's end users (physicians, technologists, and site leaders) with a single portal to interact with Merge's various services. This app acts as a route for inexpensive communication with users, incentivizes users to enroll in online learning, improves the efficiency of service requests and more.

Shipped

  • Helitack - iOS

    • Helitack is a lightweight, motion-controlled side scroller. Players pilot a firefighting helicopter to extinguish forest fires while dodging birds and buildings. We developed new motion-control and parallax scrolling systems for this game, but were able to keep total development time under one month by repurposing assets and educational content from previous games. Before we released Fight the Fire v3.0, Helitack generated the highest average revenue per user of any of our games.

  • Fight the Fire - iOS

    • Fight the Fire began as a simple wildfire spread simulation demo at a Startup Weekend. With the help of former wildland firefighter Ariel Strong, we developed it into a fullly-featured educational strategy game with 50 levels and a variety of gameplay options. Fight the Fire is Zippy Brain's most-downloaded commercial project and has been featured in the firefighting trade publication, Firehouse.

  • Beehive Blast - iOS

    • Beehive Blast is a spinoff of our previous game, Battle for the Birdhouse. It was our first game to employ local notifications to increase user retention and Facebook authentication and sharing to improve its viral coefficient.

  • Battle for the Birdhouse - iOS | Android

    • Battle for the Birdhouse is a quick, arcade-style game where users help birds get back into their birdhouse while keeping bees out. We released Battle for the Birdhouse simultaneously on the App Store and on Google Play to gain insights on the differences between the two platforms. Ultimately, we determined that it's generally more efficient to release on the App Store first, as initial exposure and average revenue per user are somewhat better than on Google Play.

  • Puznetic - iOS

    • Puznetic is a modern twist on the classic jigsaw puzzle game that includes physics and gravity effects. Puznetic is available for free on the App Store, but users can access the paid versions of Puznetic from within the application. We released the family of Puznetic games as an experiment to see if we could sell premium games by advertising them through our own free product.

  • Puznetic Pets - iOS | Nook | Kindle

    • Puznetic Pets is a standalone game that uses the same gameplay engine as Puznetic that includes a large collection of pet-themed content. Users on iOS are referred to Puznetic Pets from within the free version of Puznetic. We also released Puznetic Pets on Nook and Kindle to gain some insights on how those markets work.

  • Puznetic Sky - iOS | Nook | Kindle

    • Puznetic Sky is a standalone Puznetic game that includes educational space and astronomy themed content. As with Puznetic Pets, iOS users are referred to Puznetic Sky through the free version of Puznetic.

  • Puznetic Art - iOS | Nook | Kindle

    • Puznetic Art is the standalone Puznetic game that focuses on fine art and includes impressionist, ukiyo-e, and baroque pieces. As with Puznetic Pets and Sky, users are referred to Puznetic Art through the free version of Puznetic.

  • Dot Direct - iOS

    • Dot Direct is a geometry-styled arcade game and my first mobile game project. It includes a variety of addictive gameplay types and a progressive difficulty system.

These projects are also showcased on the homepage of ZippyBrain.com.
